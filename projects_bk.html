Multi-Armed Bandit with Switching Cost

Approaches to the multi-armed bandit problem has been studied at length. Our project was to analyze a paper by R. Agrawal, M.V Hedge, and D. Teneketzis. We formally explained the findings and assessed their research methods.

Here is a summary of what the paper outlines:

  • When there is a cost associated to switching machines, there exists an optimal strategy
  • This strategy relies on you playing as many games as possible
  • You must gradually adapt to information you gather about each machine
  • The optimal strategy achieves the same result as if there were no switching cost

Read our analysis and summary of these incredible results!

System Control as it Relates to Smart Thermostats

Given a black box system that would distort an input signal, our responsibility was to develop a system to control the output. After development, we created a formal report to outline the control system and relate this problem to the real world. We chose the control of temperature in a home.

The key components of the control system are:

  • Assessment of linearity and time-invariance
  • Apply filtering in time and frequency domain
  • Tweak gains to control poles and zeroes
